The Design Process

Problem Statement

Traditional craftsmanship is losing importance in the life of the urban population, especially the younger generation due to rapid globalisation. This happens because the crafting techniques are intricate, require patience and most of the craftsmen are located in rural areas, unaware of the contemporary design trends. We followed a 5W and 1H process to map out our problem statement.

Primary Research

For our Primary Research we conducted various User Interviews, rolled out Surveys through Google Forms and connected with experts working in similar domains to get a much more deeper and meaningful understnding of our problem. While our User and Expert interviews focused primarily at collecting subjective data, our surveys helped us gain key quantitative data about the demographics of our target audience in a concise and clean manner.

Secondary Research

Here we focused heavily on Academic Literature already published in the domains of Mixed Reality, Indian Craftsmanship, Education etc. A healthy amount of literature was reviewed to understand the work that had already been done in the domain. Also apart from the literature review we also foucsed on identifying competitors and tried to envision what a solution product should look like. We identified four competitors and plotted a Line Chart to map out the plus points and shortcomings of each competitor.

Ideation Phase

After we had gathered insights and finished up the requirement gathering we moved onto ideating some solutions to the problem at hand. Throughout the ideation process we kept on refining our Problem Statement so that we can propose and build a cohesive solution in the end. We employed various techniques such as Six Thinking Hats, Brainstorming and then shortlisted our ideas using the dot voting method all along following the 10+10 Ideation Technique. We finalised our our shortlisted ideas, drew quick sketches of the same, selected the best one and repeated the process. Such an in-depth ideation process helped us narrow down our solution and provided enough iterations for us to confidently move into the prototyping stage of the project.

Low - Fidelity Prototyping

We identified key features of the platform and decided to prototype each functionality spearately and also test out them separately on our target audience. We created three prototypes, to test out the usability and functionlity of VR Based system, importance of a self created environment and the value of a guided tutorial and a storytelling experience.
